#### **At 26, Andrew del Villar becomes CEO of DEL Records, the indie label behind Eslabon Armado and Ella Baila Sola. Inside the generational shift at regional Mexican music's most influential label.**

On November 6, 2025, Andrew del Villar walked into the DEL Records offices in Bell Gardens, California, with a title he had been preparing for most of his adult life. At 26 years old, he became the Chief Executive Officer of the independent label his father founded in 2008 - the same label that helped launch Eslabon Armado, Ariel Camacho, Gerardo Ortiz, and Lenin Ramírez, and that currently sits at over 12 million YouTube subscribers and 11 billion views.

The transition had been planned for years. Andrew had been serving as Chief Operating Officer, and before that, he had spent nearly a decade working every level of the business - from collecting tickets at nightclub doors to producing sold-out concerts at Crypto.com Arena. But the timing of the formal handoff carried weight beyond succession planning. His father, Ángel del Villar, had been sentenced to four years in federal prison after being convicted on charges related to the Foreign Narcotics Kingpin Designation Act.

**What it matters:** Andrew del Villar's appointment marks the first generational leadership change at DEL Records since its founding. At 26, he becomes one of the youngest CEOs of a major independent Latin music label - taking over an operation with hundreds of platinum and gold certifications, dozens of number-one singles, and a global publishing partnership with BMG. (Source: EIN Presswire, Billboard, November 2025)

#### **Who Is Andrew del Villar?**

Andrew del Villar was born and raised in Los Angeles, the son of DEL Records founder José Ángel del Villar. He grew up inside the business. Not adjacent to it - inside it.

He started working for the label as a teenager, roughly seven or eight years before taking the CEO role. His first job was collecting tickets and charging admission at nightclub events the label promoted. From there, he moved into booking - first theaters, then arenas - handling the logistics of putting DEL Records artists in front of live audiences across the United States.

Then came the A&R instinct. Andrew began scouting artists on social media, sending his father recommendations he discovered online. Two of those recommendations turned into signings that would reshape the label's trajectory: **Eslabon Armado** and **T3R Elemento**.

*"I started getting involved like 7 or 8 years ago and started from the bottom; collecting tickets, charging when we were doing the nightclubs, and booking U.S. shows in theaters and arenas. Then, I kinda became A&R, sending my dad artists I was finding on social media. I would say, 'Hey, what do you think about them?' Just like my dad, I have a good ear and can identify who has potential." (Source: Billboard, November 2025)*

By the time Andrew was named COO, he had already produced sold-out concerts at **Crypto.com Arena** and **Toyota Arena** - venues that seat between 10,000 and 20,000 people. The kid who started collecting tickets at nightclub doors was now running the live events operation for one of the most active touring rosters in regional Mexican music.

#### **Why Does This Leadership Transition Matter for Regional Mexican Music?**

DEL Records is not a small operation. It is one of the most consequential independent labels in Latin music history, and it built much of its influence during a period when the genre it championed - regional Mexican - went from niche to dominant.

The label launched in 2008 with one artist and a YouTube channel in a working-class neighborhood east of downtown Los Angeles. Ángel del Villar, who had previously run a fencing company, built DEL Records into a self-contained operation: label, publishing, studios, and entertainment arm, all under one roof.

The first major signing was **Gerardo Ortiz** in 2009. Ortiz earned two Grammy nominations during his decade with the label - Best Norteño Album for Ni Hoy Ni Mañana and Best Regional Mexican Music Album for El Primer Ministro. By 2013, DEL Records had added **Luis Coronel**, whose debut album entered the Billboard Regional Mexican Albums chart within three weeks of release. In 2014, the label signed **Ariel Camacho y Los Plebes del Rancho**, whose sierreño sound from Northwestern Mexico had barely registered outside Sinaloa.

Then came the Billboard Latin Music Awards. In 2016, DEL Records took home six awards, including Regional Mexican Airplay Label of the Year and Regional Mexican Airplay Imprint of the Year. In 2017, the label won five more. By that point, DEL Records had established itself as the dominant independent force in regional Mexican music.

*DEL Records has earned **hundreds of platinum and gold certifications** and **dozens of number-one singles**. The label's artists have won multiple Billboard Latin Music Awards, Lo Nuestro Awards, and iHeart Radio Music Awards. (Source: DEL Records, EIN Presswire, November 2025)*

The operation Andrew inherits is not a startup. It is a 17-year-old institution with a catalog that generates billions of views annually, a global publishing deal with BMG, and an artist development pipeline that has consistently produced chart-topping acts. The question is not whether the label can survive the transition. The question is what Andrew plans to do with it.

#### **What Made 'Ella Baila Sola' a Turning Point for the Label?**

Any article about DEL Records has to account for what happened in 2023, because "Ella Baila Sola" changed the math for the entire genre.

When Eslabon Armado and Peso Pluma released the track in March 2023, regional Mexican music had never reached the top ten of the Billboard Hot 100. No song from the genre had done it in the chart's 65-year history.

#### **"Ella Baila Sola" peaked at number four.**

It became the first regional Mexican song to top the **Billboard Global 200** - holding that position for six consecutive weeks. It spent **19 straight weeks at number one** on Hot Latin Songs. Spotify named it **Global Song of the Summer 2023**. It crossed **one billion streams on Spotify alone** - the first regional Mexican track to ever reach that number. The RIAA certified it **21 times Platinum** in the Latin field. Rolling Stone placed it at number 402 on its "500 Greatest Songs of All Time" list.

*"Ella Baila Sola" by Eslabon Armado and Peso Pluma peaked at number 4 on the Billboard Hot 100, became the first regional Mexican song to top the Billboard Global 200, and was certified 21 times Platinum by the RIAA. (Source: Billboard, RIAA, Spotify, 2023)*

Peso Pluma went on to win the Grammy for Best Música Mexicana Album. He broke Bad Bunny's record with **25 simultaneous titles** on Hot Latin Songs. He became Mexico's most-streamed artist of all time.

Andrew del Villar was part of the team that built this. He helped identify and sign Eslabon Armado when the group was still unknown, recommending them to his father after discovering the act on social media. Their debut EP, Navegando y Flotando, came out in 2019 on DEL Records. Four years later, the song they released on the label rewrote the rules for Latin music.

#### **How Is Andrew Planning to Run DEL Records Differently?**

Andrew has been clear that continuity is part of the plan - but not the whole plan.

In his first interviews after the appointment, he outlined a strategic direction that leans heavily into social media, global talent discovery, and a more focused roster. The core genre stays the same. The methods change.

*"This new era will differentiate from my dad's run because I will launch new marketing and promotion strategies, leaning heavily on social media." (Source: Billboard, November 2025)*

The most concrete initiative is the revamp of **"Canta Con Del,"** a talent discovery program his father launched roughly ten years ago. The original format was local - aspiring artists would perform at the family's restaurant in California for a shot at a record deal. Under Andrew, the program goes global and digital. Artists anywhere in the world can now submit songs and performances directly through DEL Records' social media channels.

*"There's talent everywhere and we want people to be heard and get an opportunity." (Source: Billboard, November 2025)*

Andrew has also stated his target roster size: approximately **15 artists**. In an interview on the **Agushto Papá Podcast** - the first and largest podcast dedicated to regional Mexican music - he explained the logic behind keeping the operation focused.

*"I think fifteen is a sweet spot to have signed because at the end of the day, every artist needs their attention, their time. So I think with the fifteen is more than enough to work on and develop them as well." (Source: Agushto Papá Podcast, November 11, 2025)*

Since taking the CEO title, Andrew has already signed three new acts - **Marco Granillo**, **Andi Luan**, and **Cobian Montana** - with three additional artists in the final stages of signing by year-end. The label's current active roster also includes **Lenin Ramírez**, **Yahir Saldívar**, **Sucesión M,** and **Panchito Arredondo**.

#### **What Does the Ariel Camacho Legacy Mean for Andrew's DEL Records?**

You cannot tell the DEL Records story without Ariel Camacho, and Andrew knows it.

Camacho signed to DEL Records in 2014 with Los Plebes del Rancho, carrying a sierreño sound that blended traditional Mexican instrumentation with a rawness that connected with a generation of young listeners across the U.S. border. Under the label, the group built a fanbase that turned a regional style into something much larger.

When Camacho died in a car accident on February 25, 2015, at age 22, the loss was devastating. But the music did not stop. "El Karma" reached number one on Billboard Hot Latin Songs in March 2015. The posthumous catalog continued to grow.

A full decade later, his video **"No Lo Hice Bien"** - released on the DEL Records YouTube channel on February 14, 2016 - sits at **764.8 million views**. It remains the most-watched video on the entire channel, ahead of songs by Eslabon Armado, Peso Pluma, and every other artist who has passed through the label's roster. The average view duration is **77.4%**, meaning three out of four viewers still watch the entire song, ten years after upload.

Artists like Peso Pluma, Christian Nodal, and Fuerza Regida have publicly credited Camacho with paving the way for the current generation of regional Mexican music. The roots of the 2023–2026 boom trace directly back to the sound he built on DEL Records between 2014 and 2015.

For Andrew, this legacy is both an asset and a responsibility. The catalog keeps performing. The question is whether the next generation of DEL Records artists can build something equally enduring.

#### **Where Does DEL Records Sit in the Larger Industry Picture?**

The timing of Andrew's appointment coincides with regional Mexican music's most dominant stretch in history.

According to Billboard, regional Mexican was the **second-largest growth genre** in the United States during the first half of 2025, up **32.95% year over year**. Spotify reported that Música Mexicana streams grew over **440% globally** between 2018 and 2023. Amazon Music said the genre grew **249% in 2024 alone**. Regional Mexican is now the most-streamed Latin music category in the United States, topping charts in **26 states**.

DEL Records helped build this wave. The label's YouTube channel - with over **12 million subscribers** and **11 billion lifetime views** - is among the largest regional Mexican music channels on the platform. The 28-day view average holds steady at roughly **2.1 million views per day**. The catalog spans **389 music videos, 891 Shorts**, and a monthly unique audience of **18.3 million viewers**.

*Regional Mexican music was the second-largest growth genre in the U.S. during H1 2025, growing 32.95% year over year. Spotify's Música Mexicana streams increased over 440% globally between 2018 and 2023, and Amazon Music reported 249% growth for the genre in 2024. (Source: Billboard, Spotify, Amazon Music)*

DEL Records operates independently. The label has stated publicly that it does not have partnerships with any major labels. Its global publishing administration runs through **BMG** - a relationship formalized in 2019 after an initial partnership dating to 2016 - covering DEL Melodies (BMI), DEL World Songs (ASCAP), and DEL New Music (SESAC).

Andrew takes over an operation that generates an estimated **$8.3 million in annual revenue**, with a net worth estimated between **$18 million and $25 million** based on YouTube advertising revenue and other income streams.

#### **What Has Andrew Said About His Father's Influence?**

Andrew has spoken openly about learning the business from his father, and about the mindset required to lead through a period of transition.

*"My dad has always been my role model, everything that has been thrown at him is a lot, we've had these talks for years and he's always been positive about everything. He's told me to never let things get to me because in our genre, and the industry in general, people don't wish you well. But never let the negativity get to you." (Source: Billboard, November 2025)*

The succession was not rushed. Andrew and his father had discussed the transition for years, even before the legal proceedings that accelerated the timeline. In his Agushto Papá Podcast interview, Andrew described the relationship as one built on implicit understanding.

*"I really know what he expects. He doesn't really tell me much, but I really know what he expects from me as well." (Source: Agushto Papá Podcast, November 11, 2025)*

And on the question of whether the pressure of leading a label of this scale at 26 weighs on him, Andrew was direct.

*"I'm not nervous, I'm excited because I know what we're able to bring to the table." (Source: Agushto Papá Podcast, November 11, 2025)*

#### **What Comes Next for DEL Records Under Andrew del Villar?**

The immediate priorities are clear: stabilize the roster, expand digital reach, and keep the catalog working.

Andrew's emphasis on social media is not just marketing language. DEL Records' YouTube channel already demonstrates the power of catalog-driven consumption - **69% of the channel's traffic** comes from playlists, meaning the back catalog effectively promotes itself through listening sessions. That model rewards consistency and longevity over individual viral moments, and it aligns with Andrew's stated goal of developing artists from the ground up rather than chasing quick hits.

The "Canta Con Del" revamp signals a shift in how the label finds talent. By opening submissions globally through social media, Andrew is casting a wider net than his father's restaurant-based scouting model ever could. The digital-first approach mirrors how he discovered Eslabon Armado in the first place - scrolling through social media and recognizing potential before anyone else did.

Meanwhile, the genre continues to accelerate. Half of the 50 most-listened-to songs on Spotify Mexico in 2024 were by Mexican artists - up from 14% five years earlier. Regional Mexican now regularly appears on the Billboard Hot 100, a reality that seemed impossible before 2023. DEL Records' role in building that reality is documented in the chart history.

Andrew del Villar inherits all of it - the catalog, the platform, the audience, and the responsibility. At 26, he's betting that the combination of his father's foundation and his own digital instincts can carry the label into its next chapter.

*"This is an exciting time for DEL Records. We're building upon a legacy of excellence while embracing the future - cultivating artists who represent both the roots and evolution of regional Mexican music." (Source: EIN Presswire, November 2025)*
